Deep Water in a Sleeping Volcano

Crater Lake inspires awe. Native Americans witnessed its formation 7,700 years ago, when a violent eruption triggered the collapse of a tall peak. Scientists marvel at its purity: fed by rain and snow, with a depth of 1,943 feet, Crater Lake is the deepest lake in the United States and one of the most pristine on earth.
